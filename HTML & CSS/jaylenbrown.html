Jaylen Brown is a small forward/shooting guard for the Boston Celtics. He exceptionally delivered his talents to the table for the Boston Celtics, helping them breeze through the NBA playoffs and throughout the NBA Finals. Jaylen Brown averaged 23.9 points, 5.9 rebounds and 3.3 assists in 19 games in the 2024 playoffs all throughout his creative ways of scoring the ball as well as clamping up defenders. Due to this performance, he outperformed every other player on the Boston Celtics and showed up despite other players, putting his team first before himself, which is exactly why he deserves the 2024 Bill Russel Finals MVP award after helping the Boston Celtics win a Finals series against the Dallas Mavericks 4-1, and ultimately won his first NBA championship on June 17, 2024. I look up to Jaylen Brown because not only does he have talent on the court, but also off the court. He went to college at Berkeley, focusing on his studies, and reportedly turned down an internship from NASA, but as he was an exceptionally amazing basketball player in high school, he landed a spot on the college team. NBA Scouts suggested he was too smart to play in the NBA, but Jaylen Brown proved them wrong, he showed that you can be amazing in all aspects of life if you work hard enough.
